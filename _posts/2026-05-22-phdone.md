I got my PhD. I submitted my thesis, commencement is finished, I packed my stuff to go back to Korea.
This post is my PhD (or pre-PhD) diary.
I don't know why I'm writing this - I feel like I have to.

## First year

So I started my PhD in 2018 Fall. Now it is 2026 Spring, and you'll see soon why it took me so long (you can guess if you're Korean).
Until third year, I haven't thought about studying abroad. The major event changed my thought was the [Fields Undergraduate Summer Research Program](https://www.fields.utoronto.ca/generalinfo/Fields-Undergraduate-Summer-Research-Program), which is a summer REU at Fields Institute in Toronto. I attended it when I was a senior, and it made me realized that I might be better if I study abroad and meet more people in the world.
But it was a bit late to apply for PhD, so I decided to do masters first at POSTECH.
I applied to 15 schools (I wanted to apply to more, like 30-ish, but my English was poor and so was TOEFL score) and got into Berkeley. The admission email was in a spam folder and I found it a week after it arrived (so anyone who is applying PhD or whatsoever, *please check your spam folder*).

I really enjoyed my first year. I took some courses and learned a lot (and didn't do well oops I got two B's).
My favorite class was David Nadler's Lie Group course, where he explain the subject in a very intuitive and geometric way. The exams were more interesting; I still remember that the exams were all short-answer questions, and one of them gave us a list of 10 Lie groups and ask to group them that share the same Lie algebra (you can find it [here](https://math.berkeley.edu/~nadler/2018.math261a.midterm2.pdf)).
But more importantly, all my cohorts were really nice and very passionate about mathematics. Sometime we talk about math endlessly, and I was really happy with that. Also, Berkeley is close to San Francisco, so I could attend a lot of concerts, which was good for my mental health.
I also once went to Muse concert with bunch of my cohorts.

## Alternative Military Service, Covid, etc

Unfortunately, I had to go back to Korea for alternative military service in 2019. 
Since I don't want to think about qualifying exam after I come back 3 years later, I took it at the end of my first year. Berkeley's QE is oral exam, where student choose 3 topics (two major and one minor), and I choose Automorphic Forms, Number Theory, and Complex Analysis (Minor). I passed the exam, although I think I didn't do well in Automorphic Forms. You can find my QE syllabus and questions [here](https://seewoo5.github.io/assets/Qual_report.pdf).

After I came back to Korea, I started to find a company to work on. For a context, I (or many other people) have two choices: go to the actual military service (1.5 years), or work at a company that is approved by the government for alternative military service (3 years).
The first one is much shorter, but people may prefer the second one because they can do something more interesting.
I took the second path, and I eventually got a job at [Riiid](https://corp.socra.ai/), an Ed-tech company that uses AI to help students learn better (the name of the company changed though). I worked there for almost two years, developing deep learning models for student performance prediction (which is usually called "knowledge tracing" in the literature) and recommendation systems. 
In fact, it was my first time to learn about deep learning, and I learned a lot about it. It was also a time when BERT, GPT2, Reformer, etc. came out and people surprised.
At the moment, it was even possible to search all papers that are accepted to ICML which uses transformer, and read them all (at least the abstract, which I did) - but now it simply doesn't make sense because every paper uses transformer.
Also, I learned about reinforcement learning and [Adam Wagner's work](https://arxiv.org/abs/2104.14516) on finding counterexample for conjectures in graph theory using RL, which was the first paper I read on "AI for Mathematics".

I moved to another company, called [CryptoLab](https://www.cryptolab.co.kr/en/home/). It is a completely different company that does homomorphic encryption and use it for private machine learning.
I decided to move to this company because it looks more mathematical, especially considering how the homomorphic encryption (or lattice-based cryptography) is related to number theory.
Eventually, I worked on developing a python library that uses our homomorphic encryption library (called HEAAN, based on CKKS scheme), which was eventually algorithm design tasks.
In summary, you have a limited operations on vectors of fixed length (component-wise addition and multiplication, and rotation), and you want to implement ML algorithms using small number of operations (especially multiplication and rotation).
In some sense, I could even do this withouth knowing much about CKKS scheme itself, and luckily I was able to write [a paper](https://proceedings.mlr.press/v202/lee23m/lee23m.pdf) based on the work.

All of this happened in 2019 - 2022, which was also the time when Covid-19 pandemic started. 
Sometimes I had to work at home, and it was not much fun since I couldn't meet my colleagues in person.
But anyway, the situation got better and 3 years passed.
